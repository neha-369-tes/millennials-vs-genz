Millennials vs Gen Z

Different Vibes, Same Deadlines

A lighthearted look at two generations navigating modern life

From avocado toast to TikTok trends, let's explore what makes these generations tick

Millennials (1981-1996)
Gen Z (1997-2012)
Presented by: Speaker 1
Slide 1 - Introduction
Communication Styles
Presented by: Speaker 2

Millennials

  • Writes full emails with proper grammar and "Best regards"
  • Uses Facebook and Instagram religiously
  • Prefers phone calls for important conversations
  • Still uses complete sentences in texts
  • Emoticons > Emojis (sometimes)

Gen Z

  • Masters the art of saying everything in 3 words or less
  • TikTok comments are their natural habitat
  • Would rather eat glass than make a phone call
  • "no cap fr fr" is proper grammar
  • Communicates entirely through memes and GIFs
Slide 2 - Communication Styles
Technology Relationship
Presented by: Speaker 3

Millennials

  • Remembers dial-up internet and feels ancient
  • Had to learn social media as adults
  • Still has a Facebook account they rarely use
  • Knows what a CD is and owned hundreds
  • Thinks smartphones are magical but manageable

Gen Z

  • Born with a smartphone in their hand
  • Can navigate any app within 30 seconds
  • Has never known a world without high-speed internet
  • Thinks millennials are old for using Facebook
  • Creates viral TikTok dances in their sleep
Slide 3 - Technology Relationship
Work Culture
Presented by: Speaker 4

Millennials

  • Wants work-life balance but replies to emails at 11 PM
  • Pioneered the "hustle culture" mentality
  • Loves open office spaces (regrets it now)
  • Meetings about meetings about meetings
  • Still believes in climbing the corporate ladder

Gen Z

  • Strict boundaries: work phone off after 5 PM
  • Side hustles are the main hustle
  • Remote work is not a perk, it's a requirement
  • "That could have been an email" energy
  • Would rather be an influencer than a manager
Slide 4 - Work Culture
Financial Habits
Presented by: Speaker 5

Millennials

  • Blames avocado toast for not owning a house
  • Has 47 different budgeting apps
  • Subscription services are slowly draining their bank account
  • Shops at Target for "one thing" - leaves with $200 receipt
  • Retirement planning started at 35

Gen Z

  • Invests in crypto and stocks via meme advice
  • Thrift shopping is both trendy and necessary
  • Knows they'll never afford a house, plans accordingly
  • Splits every expense with friends via Venmo
  • Financial advice comes from TikTok videos
Slide 5 - Financial Habits
Social Media Usage
Presented by: Speaker 6

Millennials

  • Carefully curates Instagram with Valencia filter
  • Writes full paragraphs as Facebook statuses
  • LinkedIn posts about "grinding" and "hustle"
  • Screenshots tweets to share on Instagram Stories
  • Still has photos from 2009 on their profile

Gen Z

  • Posts unfiltered selfies with "main character energy"
  • BeReal notifications cause instant panic
  • TikTok algorithm knows them better than their parents
  • Creates 47 different Instagram accounts for different vibes
  • Treats Twitter like a 24/7 comedy show
Slide 6 - Social Media Usage
Entertainment Preferences
Presented by: Speaker 7

Millennials

  • Binge-watches entire Netflix series in one weekend
  • Still quotes The Office religiously
  • Owns every streaming service but watches YouTube
  • Nostalgic for 90s/early 2000s everything
  • Podcast addiction is real and concerning

Gen Z

  • Watches TikTok for 6 hours straight without blinking
  • Attention span requires content to be under 60 seconds
  • Discovers new music through 15-second clips
  • Makes 2000s trends cool again (Y2K revival)
  • Prefers vertical videos over horizontal
Slide 7 - Entertainment Preferences
Fashion & Lifestyle
Presented by: Speaker 8

Millennials

  • Skinny jeans are a personality trait
  • Side parts and hair straighteners for life
  • Starbucks orders are unnecessarily complicated
  • Athleisure is acceptable formal wear
  • Plants everywhere but somehow still kills them

Gen Z

  • Mom jeans and crop tops are the uniform
  • Middle parts with curtain bangs or bust
  • Iced coffee year-round, even in blizzards
  • Thrift store finds mixed with designer pieces
  • Sustainable living is non-negotiable
Slide 8 - Fashion & Lifestyle
Dating & Relationships
Presented by: Speaker 9

Millennials

  • Swipes right on dating apps while watching Netflix
  • Relationship status: "It's complicated" with life
  • Dates involve carefully planned Instagram moments
  • Ghosting is an art form they perfected
  • Wants marriage but terrified of commitment

Gen Z

  • Finds love through TikTok comments and Discord
  • Relationship goals: emotional intelligence required
  • Dates are casual hangouts with good vibes
  • Communication is key - no toxic behaviors allowed
  • Marriage? Maybe. Happiness? Definitely.
